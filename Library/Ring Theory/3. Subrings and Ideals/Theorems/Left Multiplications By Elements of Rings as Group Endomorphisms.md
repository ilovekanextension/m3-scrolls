**Theorem.** Let $R$ be a ring. Then, for all $a\in R$, the left-multiplication function $$a\cdot-:R\to R$$is a group homomorphism on $(R,+)$.

**Proof.** Suppose $x,y\in R$. We have
$$\begin{align}
a\cdot-(x+y) & =a\cdot(x+y) \\
 & =a\cdot x+a\cdot y \\
 & =a\cdot-(x)+a\cdot-(y).\blacksquare
\end{align}$$
